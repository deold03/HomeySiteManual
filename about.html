About

I'm Danny. I host Chicago short-term rentals like they're my own — because one of them is.

Founder of Homey Homes & Co. Eleven-year Airbnb Superhost. Licensed Chicago realtor. Operator of a 4.99-star flagship in Logan Square.

11+ years hosting 4.99★ Superhost 160+ five-star reviews Top 1% of Airbnb homes
How I got here 01

The long way around to short-term rentals.

I listed my first Airbnb in 2013, out of a Brooklyn apartment. I traveled a lot, the place sat empty when I wasn't there, and hosting it was a way to subsidize the trips. I had no idea it would become the through-line of the next decade.

I started my career at Goldman Sachs, where I spent four years in real estate investing, and then moved into corporate strategy for another eight. Two very different vantage points on how real estate and businesses actually make money — one from the institutional side, one from inside operating companies figuring out where to put their resources. I learned a lot. I also learned that corporate life wasn't for me.

In 2019 I bought a 1906 graystone in Logan Square, restored it, and started hosting it the way I'd always wanted to host — with real design, real hospitality, and a real person behind the messages. It's now a 4.99-star listing with more than 160 reviews and sits in the top 1% of Airbnb homes nationwide. That property is the reason this business exists.

Why I built Homey Homes 02

Because somebody had to do it differently.

The more I hosted, the more I realized this was the work I actually wanted to be doing. Real estate, design, hospitality — three things I genuinely care about, all in one job. But I also started paying attention to what was available to other local hosts who didn't want to do it themselves, and the only real option was a large property management company.

I travel a lot. I've stayed in plenty of places run by big managers, and I've felt the difference from the guest side too. It's cold. It's transactional. It's a chatbot until something goes wrong, and then it's a dispatcher reading from a script. That's not what hosting is supposed to feel like — for the guest or for the owner.

So I built the version I'd want on both sides of it. One transparent fee. One person — me — who actually knows your property and answers when you text. Warmer than a corporation. More like texting a friend who happens to know what they're doing.

What I bring 03

Three things, one operator.

Operator's eye

Eleven years of hosting means I've already made most of the mistakes. I know what guests complain about before they complain, what breaks first, and what's worth spending money on versus what isn't.

Investor's lens

Four years at Goldman in real estate, plus a Chicago realtor's license, means I think about your property as an asset — not just a listing. Revenue strategy, neighborhood viability, when to hold and when to reposition.

Designer's standards

I design every space I host, and I've taken on standalone design projects from Chicago to Miami. Spaces that photograph beautifully tend to also be spaces that earn five stars. That's not a coincidence.

Working together 04

Small on purpose.

I personally oversee every property I host. I manage calendar and pricing strategy myself. I handle guest communications — including the 2am ones. When something needs fixing, I refer you to vendors I've worked with and trust, and coordinate the introduction so you're not starting cold.

I don't take on more than I can host the way I host my own. If that means turning down work, I turn down work.
